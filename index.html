Understanding Sharia Law

What is Sharia Law?

Sharia is Islam’s legal framework for moral guidance and legal principles derived covering all aspects of life:

  • Worship: Prayer, fasting, zakat, pilgrimage
  • Personal Life: Food, dress, marriage, ethics
  • Social/Commerce: Business, inheritance, contracts, crimes

interpreted by scholars in context (faqih, mujtahid) to adapt to varying circumstances.

Primary & Secondary Sources

Quran

  • Exact words of God
  • 114 surahs in classical Arabic
  • Legal verses (ayat) comprise 14% content

Sunnah

  • Authentic hadeeths compiled in collections
  • Clarifies and details Qur'anic injunctions
  • Includes actions, approvals, and inactions of the Prophet

Ijma (Scholarly) Consensus

Applications in:

  • Contemporary medical ethics (bioethics, etc.)
  • Financial transactions (Islamic banking rulings)
  • New technological challenges

Analogical Reasoning

  • Applies existing laws to new circumstances
  • Derivation of similar causes (illah)
  • Used in modern jurisprudence (fiqh muasar)

Madhhabs (Schools of Thought)

Maliki School

Founded by Imam Malik ibn Anas (d.795)

  • Dominant in North Africa
  • Primacy given to Medinan practices
  • Strong emphasis on public interest (maslaha)
  • Employs (block evas) to prevent harm

Common Misconceptions

Sharia covers all aspects of life including charity, prayer, marriage, business ethics, inheritance and more. Criminal law is a small portion.

Many Islamic scholars argue that core Sharia principles align with democratic values like justice, consultation and protection of rights.
